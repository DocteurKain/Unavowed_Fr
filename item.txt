The Bā Jiāo Guǐ (香蕉鬼) which literraly means "Banana Ghost" is often described as a female spirit that lives in babana trees.
In some folk tales from Malaysia, Thailand and Singapore, people who are greedy will summon the Bā Jiāo Guǐ for some lucky numbers in hope of winning a large amount of money.
They tie a red ribbon around the tree trunk and then tie the often end of ribbon to their beds. Then they drink a strong sleeping potion to send them into a dreamless slumber.
During sleep, the spirit appears and offers a set of winning numbers to caster in exchange for being set free.
If the person does not fulfill his/her promise to set the ghost free after winning, his or her family will be cursed in this life and the next.
This spirit is often likened to the Pontianak in Malay folklore.
